The whole concept of fasting is as important in Islam as in any other religion, I could even say it is more important in Islam than in any other
religion. In Islam a whole month has been dedicated to fasting which just shows how important it is. Islam believes that fasting is just not observed
by not eating food but preventing one from engaging in acts like:

  • All forms of evil acts
  • Feasting one's eyes on inappropriate things
  • Listening to gossip and backbites
  • Meeting with opposite sex
  • Among Others

If anyone engages in any of the above acts, then the person has automatically invalidated his or her fast.

Apart from the 30 days fasting, Islam also has other fastings such as:

  • Optional fasting
  • Fasting on the Day of Arafat
  • Additional fasting after Ramadan
  • Among other

Fasting in Islam is observed starting just before sunrise and ends immediately after sunset. In Islam fasting observed to;

  • Seek Allah's forgiveness
  • Obtain Mercy from Allah
  • Gain amnesty from Allah against the Great Fire
  • Acquire blessing from Allah
